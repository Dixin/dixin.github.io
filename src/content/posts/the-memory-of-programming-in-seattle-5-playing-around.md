## Green Lake

[Green Lake](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Lake_\(Seattle\)) is a freshwater lake in north central [Seattle](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle,_Washington). It is a beacon for wildlife. Many types of wildlife, [ducks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duck), [cormorants](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cormorant), [loons](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loon), [herons](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heron), [geese](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goose), [turtles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turtle), [raccoons](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raccoon), [rats](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rat), [squirrels](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Squirrel), [bats](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bat), [hawks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawk), [eagles](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eagle), and [osprey](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osprey) are among the wild creatures commonly viewed there.

## Mount Rainier

[Mount Rainier](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Rainier) is a very famous snow mountain. I first knew it in 2004, from the movie “[Alien vs. Predator](http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0370263/)”:

![alien-vs-predator_thumb](https://aspblogs.z22.web.core.windows.net/dixin/Media/alienvspredator_thumb_515ACD12.jpg "alien-vs-predator_thumb")

![alien-vs-predator-snapshot_thumb](https://aspblogs.z22.web.core.windows.net/dixin/Media/alienvspredatorsnapshot_thumb_01FDAEF1.jpg "alien-vs-predator-snapshot_thumb")

Mount Rainier is an active volcano in [Pierce County](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierce_County,_Washington), located 54 miles (87 km) southeast of [Seattle](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle,_Washington). Driving from Bellevue to the mountain costs at least 4 hours. The mountain is very beautiful, surrounded and protected within [Mount Rainier National Park](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Rainier_National_Park):

## Snoqualmie Falls

[Snoqualmie Falls](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snoqualmie_Falls) is a 82m waterfall on the [Snoqualmie River](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snoqualmie_River) between [Snoqualmie](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snoqualmie,_Washington) and [Fall City](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_City,_Washington). It is one of Washington's most popular scenic attractions. More than 1.5 million visitors come to the Falls every year.
